Abandon In Time;

Making A Self-Help Journal During the Pandemic

I created Abandon In Time; amid the pandemic, as a journal meant to help those sorting through their feelings of fear, abandonment, and loneliness. Each page features an interactive prompt to provoke memories and encourage documentation. Activities include drawing, writing, or collecting— all of which are tools used in art therapy. Derived from Emotion-Focused Therapy (EMT), it proposes the centrality of emotion in meaning-making and adaptive behavior. Q: What does it mean to be abandoned?​​​​​​​ At its core, abandonment refers to an absence in responsibility that changes the status quo. Once it's happened, it destabilizes what was known, resulting in an emotional and/or physical withdrawal. The pain lingers in fragmented emotions, like pieces of a disassembled puzzle with no guide. Time, care, and attention are needed to smooth down old splinters, but can also expose even older wounds. By examining our abandoned spaces, we acknowledge emotion, thought, and healing processes. In the spirit of community and kinship, I hope to create a functioning website that centers around collecting, displaying, and sharing answers publicly. Submitted stories would be displayed for all to read—whether they be positive, negative, or anything in between. Sharing these moments can provide solace or validation, especially to those wanting anonymity. A visual walkthrough of the website and its functions.
